Why attendance matters more than you think

Attendance is the strongest predictor of academic success. A student who misses 10% of school days in a term is significantly more likely to fall behind. Yet most schools still track attendance with paper registers that are rarely analysed and never reach parents in real-time.

SchoolPadi makes attendance a live, visual system — not a dusty register.

Daily roll call from any device

Teachers open their class roster on a phone, tablet, or laptop and mark each student as Present, Absent, or Late. The interface is optimised for speed — a full class of 40 students takes under two minutes.

  • Mobile-first — Teachers mark attendance on the move, even from the classroom
  • One student per day — The system enforces one attendance record per student per date, preventing duplicates
  • Instant save — Records are available immediately to administrators and parents

Monthly heatmaps that reveal patterns

The calendar heatmap is where SchoolPadi transforms raw data into insight. Each day is colour-coded — black for present, red for absent, yellow for late — creating a visual pattern that teachers and parents can read at a glance.

Patterns like "absent every Monday" or "late three days in a row" become immediately obvious. This helps teachers intervene before small issues become chronic absenteeism.

Automated parent notifications

When a student is marked absent without prior explanation, SchoolPadi can automatically notify the parent via the parent portal. Parents receive a clear message: "Ama was marked absent today." No phone call needed. No end-of-term surprise.

This real-time loop between school and home changes the dynamic entirely. Parents become partners in attendance, not passive observers.

School-wide attendance dashboard

Administrators see attendance across the entire school from a single view:

  • Class-level rates — Which classes have the best and worst attendance?
  • Chronic absentees — Students falling below the acceptable threshold
  • Teacher compliance — Are all teachers marking attendance daily?
  • Trend reports — Compare attendance rates month over month and term over term
