Technology that serves teachers

AI in education works best when it takes over the repetitive tasks — creating questions, marking assessments, answering routine queries — so teachers can spend their energy on what matters: actual teaching.

SchoolPadi's AI tools are built with this principle. They're not flashy gimmicks. They're practical systems that save teachers hours every week.

AI homework generation

A teacher selects a topic and difficulty level. SchoolPadi's AI generates a complete set of homework questions — multiple choice, short answer, or structured — aligned to the GES curriculum for the selected class level.

  • Topic-based — Teacher sets the subject, strand, and difficulty
  • GES-aligned — Questions follow the Ghana Education Service curriculum indicators
  • Difficulty levels — Easy, medium, and hard questions to challenge all students
  • Instant generation — A full assignment in under 30 seconds

Teachers review, adjust if needed, and publish the homework to their class. Students complete it online through the SchoolPadi student portal.

Auto-marking and grade push

When students submit their homework online, SchoolPadi's AI marks the responses automatically. Objective questions (multiple choice, fill-in-the-blank) are graded instantly. The scores are pushed directly to the grade book — no teacher intervention required.

Teachers get a summary: how the class performed, which questions were hardest, and which students need follow-up. This turns homework from a grading burden into a diagnostic tool.

GES curriculum browser

SchoolPadi includes a complete, browsable GES curriculum for core subjects. Teachers can explore strands, sub-strands, and indicators — then generate lesson plans or homework directly from any curriculum point.

Individual teachers can access this through the free curriculum browser, which lets anyone explore the GES curriculum and generate one AI lesson plan at no cost.

Platform assistant — 24/7 support

The SchoolPadi AI assistant answers questions about the platform itself. Staff can ask "How do I assign fees?" or "Where do I find student report cards?" and get immediate, accurate guidance.

This reduces support tickets, shortens the learning curve for new staff, and means schools never have to wait for business hours to get help.

What AI doesn't replace

SchoolPadi's AI is deliberately focused on administrative tasks. It doesn't replace the relationship between teacher and student. It doesn't make pedagogical decisions. It generates questions and marks objective answers — but the teaching, the mentoring, the encouragement? That stays human.

The goal is simple: give teachers their time back so they can use it where it matters most.
